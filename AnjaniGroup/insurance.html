Most Asked Questions

1. How old do I have to be to buy an Insurance?

Typically, you need to be at least 18 years old to purchase insurance independently. However, younger individuals may be covered under a parent or guardian's policy. Specific age requirements can vary depending on the type of insurance and local regulations, so it's best to check with insurance providers or speak with a licensed agent for more information tailored to your situation.

2. What is KidCare Insurance?

KidCare Insurance is a specialized type of insurance designed to provide financial protection and support for children. It typically offers coverage for various aspects of a child's well-being, including healthcare, education, and future financial needs. KidCare Insurance policies may include provisions such as health insurance coverage, education savings plans, and life insurance benefits tailored to children's needs. The goal of KidCare Insurance is to ensure that children have access to necessary resources and support, safeguarding their future and providing peace of mind for parents or guardians.

4. How do Insurance plans work?

Insurance plans offer financial protection against unexpected events or losses in exchange for regular premium payments. You select a policy that suits your needs and budget, paying premiums to the insurance company. If you experience a covered loss, you file a claim, and if approved, the insurer provides compensation to help you recover. Insurance works by pooling risks, with premiums from many policyholders contributing to a fund used to pay out claims. It's a way to mitigate financial uncertainty and protect yourself or your assets from unforeseen circumstances.

5. Who is eligible for the Future Insurance plan?

The eligibility criteria for a Future Insurance plan may vary depending on the specific plan and the insurance provider. However, generally, individuals who are of legal age and meet certain health requirements may be eligible to apply for a Future Insurance plan. Some insurance plans may have age restrictions or other criteria, so it's essential to check with the insurance provider for specific eligibility requirements. Additionally, certain plans may be designed for specific demographics, such as parents looking to secure their children's future or individuals planning for retirement. Therefore, eligibility can vary based on the type of Future Insurance plan and its intended purpose.

6. Why should I have Insurance?

Insurance provides essential financial protection against unexpected events, offering peace of mind, compliance with legal requirements, asset protection, access to healthcare, and effective risk management.
